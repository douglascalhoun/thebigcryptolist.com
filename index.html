The Big Crypto List

concise explanations of everything blockchain & web3

Blockchains are shared ledgers of cryptographically-authenticated transactions, verified and updated by peer-to-peer networks of users. Often used to store and transfer value in the form of a native on-chain currency, or to register and invoke blocks of code.

Bitcoin is the oldest and best-known decentralized digital currency, but has limited programmability. It exists entirely on its own user-run, proof-of-work blockchain.

Launched: Jan 2009
Founder: Satoshi Nakamoto (pseudonym)
Token: Bitcoin

Features:

  • Any internet-connected computer may become a network participant by running free, open-source “bitcoin node” software.
  • Nodes compete to process and record blocks of cryptographically-signed transactions (transferring Bitcoin from one account to another) onto the end of a continuously-updated, shared public ledger (the blockchain). video
  • Nodes are rewarded with newly-minted Bitcoin, which are often, in practice, sold into the open market to cover the cost of energy and computer hardware.
  • A “Bitcoin account” is simply a string of 26-35 alphanumeric characters derived from passing a chosen 256 bit “private key” through a one-way hashing function. This process is also used to “sign transactions” transferring away bitcoin owned by the account.
  • All accounts own zero Bitcoin by default, but can receive them from transactions initiated by other Bitcoin-owning accounts, or as a block reward for being a mining node.
  • Several major US financial institutions, including BlackRock, U.S. Bank and Bank of New York Mellon offer their clients exposure to or custody of digital assets including Bitcoin.

Criticisms:

  • The collective environmental impact of running bitcoin node software is significant, due to the energy-intensive proof-of-work algorithm used to secure the network.
  • Bitcoins are essentially uncollateralized assets, backed only by the faith of its users, and has been described as an economic bubble by at least eight recipients of the Nobel Memorial Prize in Economic Sciences.
  • 24 countries, including China, have banned or seriously curtailed the use of Bitcoin.
  • The bitcoin network is technically antiquated, does not allow for smart contract functionality, and its conservative community has proven resistant to innovation.
Ethereum, the original "smart contract" blockchain, provides not only a network-native cryptocurrency, but also allows for the registration and execution of on-chain code.

Launched: July 2015
Founder: Vitalik Buterin
Token: Ether

Features:

  • Developers write and deploy bundles of stored data and callable functions (contracts), which live at their own on-chain address and can receive, store and send Ether.
  • Externally Owned Accounts are simply equivalent on-chain addresses controlled directly by private keys. They can receive, store, and send Ether, but do not contain stored data or callable code.
  • Ethereum network participants process blocks of cryptographically-signed transactions, not only transferring Ether from account to account, but also triggering function calls to deployed contract code.
  • The Sept 2022 switch to a proof-of-stake consensus model increased energy efficiency and lowered inflation.

Criticisms:

  • The Ethereum protocol is still under active development, and its progress has been criticized as slower than more nimble, upstart rivals.
  • Ethereum transactions are slower and more expensive than many rival Layer 1 blockchains.
  • While praised for transparency and openness to community participation, Ethereum’s core development team maintains a degree of centralized control over the protocol.
Solana is an innovative smart contract blockchain known for fast, inexpensive transactions, but centralized due to hardware requirements and the economics of its delegated staking model.

Launched: March 2020
Founder: Anatoly Yakovenko (veteran Qualcomm engineer)
Token: SOL

Features:

  • 2,000 (live) to 50,000 (tested) transactions per second vs Ethereum’s current ~10 t/s, enabled by a series of 8 technical innovations.
  • Proof-of-history mechanism allows for time-stamped transactions, reducing communication overhead between nodes.
  • Parallel processing runtime allows transactions on discrete pieces of global state to be run concurrently.

Criticisms:

  • 60% of SOL tokens were allocated to investors & insiders at launch
  • Demanding hardware and bandwidth requirements, push stakers towards using AWS and other data centers to host validator nodes, creating points of failure / control.
  • Centralization of stakers means currently the top 30 (of ~1800) validators control a third of the network and could coordinate to halt execution.
  • There have been several network outages due to both overloaded transaction volumes and deliberate denial of service attacks that required a high-degree of validator coordination to restart the network.
BNB Chain is an Ethereum fork, controlled by the Binance cryptocurrency exchange, know for beginner accessibility, cheap transactions, and playing unwilling host to an endless parade of pump-and-dump scam tokens.

Launched: September 2020
Founder: Binance Corporation
Token: BNB

Features:

  • Efficient (but centralized) Proof of Staked Authority consensus model, allows coin holders to stake BNB coins with 21 approved validators.
  • Users receive discounted fees when trading on the Binance centralized cryptocurrency exchange platform.

Criticisms:

  • Centralized consensus model, with authorized validators chosen by Binance.
  • Has a reputation for hosting pump-and-dump scam tokens.
Avalanche is an EVM-compatible, proof-of-stake blockchain spun out of Cornell University emphasizing fault-tolerance, democratic development and an inclusive consensus model.

Launched: September 2020
Founders: Cornell University Researchers
Token: AVAX

Features:

  • Consists of three distinct chains: one for exchanging assets, one for hosting smart contracts, and one for coordinating validators.
  • 4,500 transactions per second (vs Ethereum's 14 t/s) with ~1 second finality, enabled by a unique protocol sampling sub-sets of validators.
  • Sub-nets offer app-specific chain customization while retaining interoperability.

Criticisms:

  • Has struggled with user-adoption in the face of larger rivals like Ethereum and Solana.
Cardano is a controversial Ethereum alternative, founded in 2017 by Ethereum's estranged ex-CEO, Charles Hoskinson.

Launched: Sept 2017
Founder: Charles Hoskinson
Token: ADA

Features:

  • Early implementer of energy-efficient proof-of-stake consensus with proprietary Ouroboros protocol.

Criticisms:

  • Cardano advocates are considered somewhat insular from the wider crypto community and the project has failed to gain wide adoption vs better-known Ethereum.

Decentralized Applications are smart contracts, deployed on one or more blockchains, that can operate autonomously to provide some function or utility to end users or other applications. DApps often distribute tokens that represent ownership or governance rights.

Decentralized Content Delivery Networks store user & application data in a decentralized manner, hosted by incentivized network participants running specialized software. Data may be publicly available (like a website) or cryptographically secured.

Stablecoins are on-chain currencies whose price tracks an underlying reference asset (often US Dollars). Useful for on-chain payments or held as cash-equivalent reserve assets. Either collateralized by real-world assets or algorithmically-pegged (dangerous!).

Dai is a stablecoin backed by the MakerDAO community governance system and treasury. Users deposit Ether or another cryptocurrency at a 150% collateralization ratio in order to withdraw USD-pegged DAI tokens.

Launched: Dec 2017
Developers: Maker Foundation
Token: DAI

Decentralized Lending Platforms are deployed smart contracts that allow users to lend and borrow cryptocurrencies using a variety on and off-chain mechanics for collateralization and repayment.

Aave is a decentralized liquidity market protocol allowing token-lenders to receive interest for providing single-block and perpetual loans.

Launched: Nov 2017
Token: AAVE

Compound is an algorithmic money market protocol that allows depositors to lending pools to earn interest on their capital.

Launched: Sept 2018
Token: COMP

Decentralized Exchanges, or DEXs, are deployed smart contract code that allows users to swap cryptocurrencies without an intermediary.

Uniswap is a series of smart contracts that allow the exchange between pairs of tokens based on the Ethereum blockchain.

Launched: Nov 2018
Developer: Hayden Adams
Token: UNI

dYdX is a decentralized trading platform for crypto assets, offering perpetual, margin and spot trading.

Launched: Dec 2017
Token: dYdX

Curve uses automated market maker algorithms to efficiently swap externally-pegged assets like stablecoins and Ethereum-based Bitcoin tokens.
Pancake Swap is a DEX built on the Binance BNB blockchain. It originated as one of the various forks of Ethereum-based Uniswap.

Cross-Chain Bridges allow users and smart contracts to pass messages and digital assets between blockchains.
